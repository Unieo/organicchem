Introduction to Alcohols

Alcohols are organic compounds containing one or more hydroxyl (-OH) functional groups attached to a carbon atom.

Definition

General formula: R-OH where R is an alkyl or substituted alkyl group.

The hydroxyl group makes alcohols polar molecules, capable of hydrogen bonding, which significantly affects their physical and chemical properties.

Classification of Alcohols

By Number of -OH Groups

Types Based on -OH Groups

  • Monohydric: One -OH group (e.g., CH₃CH₂OH)
  • Dihydric: Two -OH groups (e.g., HO-CH₂-CH₂-OH)
  • Trihydric: Three -OH groups (e.g., C₃H₅(OH)₃)
  • Polyhydric: Multiple -OH groups

By Carbon Atom Type

Primary, Secondary & Tertiary Alcohols

  • Primary (1°): -OH attached to a primary carbon (one other carbon)
  • Secondary (2°): -OH attached to a secondary carbon (two other carbons)
  • Tertiary (3°): -OH attached to a tertiary carbon (three other carbons)

Nomenclature of Alcohols

IUPAC Rules

1. Identify longest carbon chain containing the -OH group
2. Replace -e with -ol in parent alkane name
3. Number chain to give -OH group lowest possible number
4. Indicate position of -OH with locant

Examples

CH₃CH₂OH → Ethanol
CH₃CH(OH)CH₃ → Propan-2-ol
CH₂=CH-CH₂OH → Prop-2-en-1-ol

Physical Properties

Hydrogen Bonding Effects

Alcohols have higher boiling points than comparable hydrocarbons due to intermolecular hydrogen bonding.

Boiling Point Comparison

  • Ethane (C₂H₆): -88.6°C
  • Methanol (CH₃OH): 64.7°C
  • Ethanol (C₂H₅OH): 78.4°C

Solubility in water decreases as alkyl chain length increases due to the hydrophobic effect.

Preparation Methods

Hydration of Alkenes

Acid-Catalyzed Hydration

CH₂=CH₂ + H₂O → CH₃CH₂OH
Conditions: H₃PO₄ catalyst, 300°C, 60-70 atm

Reduction of Carbonyl Compounds

Aldehyde Reduction

R-CHO + 2[H] → R-CH₂OH
Reducing Agents: LiAlH₄, NaBH₄, H₂/Pd

Grignard Synthesis

Reaction with Formaldehyde

R-Mg-X + H-CHO → R-CH₂OH
Conditions: Dry ether, followed by H₃O⁺ workup

Chemical Reactions

Oxidation Reactions

Primary alcohols oxidize to aldehydes and carboxylic acids.

Ethanol to Acetaldehyde

CH₃CH₂OH + [O] → CH₃CHO + H₂O
Conditions: K₂Cr₂O₇/H₂SO₄, heat
Important Safety Note

Always add acid to water, never water to acid. The reaction is exothermic.

Common Oxidizing Agents

  • Potassium dichromate (K₂Cr₂O₇)
  • Pyridinium chlorochromate (PCC)
  • Potassium permanganate (KMnO₄)
  • Swern oxidation

Dehydration Reactions

Formation of Alkenes

CH₃CH₂OH → CH₂=CH₂ + H₂O
Conditions: Conc. H₂SO₄, 170°C

Esterification

Fischer Esterification

R-COOH + R'-OH → R-COO-R' + H₂O
Conditions: Acid catalyst (H₂SO₄), heat

Haloform Reaction

Iodoform Test for CH₃CH(OH)- Group

CH₃CH₂OH + 4I₂ + 6NaOH → CHI₃ + HCOONa + 5NaI + 5H₂O
Conditions: I₂ in NaOH, warm

Applications of Alcohols

Industrial Uses

  • Methanol: Formaldehyde production, antifreeze, fuel
  • Ethanol: Beverages, solvent, fuel (gasohol)
  • Isopropyl alcohol: Disinfectant, rubbing alcohol
  • Ethylene glycol: Antifreeze in automobiles
Biological Importance

Ethanol fermentation: C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂

Performed by yeast under anaerobic conditions.
