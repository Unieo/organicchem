Alcohols

Hydroxy compounds and their chemical properties, reactions, and applications

Introduction to Alcohols

Alcohols are organic compounds that contain one or more hydroxyl (-OH) functional groups attached to a saturated carbon atom.

General Formula

The general formula for alcohols is R-OH, where R represents an alkyl group.

Alcohols are classified based on the number of hydroxyl groups and the type of carbon atom to which the -OH group is attached.

Physical Properties

Alcohols exhibit unique physical properties due to hydrogen bonding between molecules.

  • Higher boiling points than corresponding alkanes
  • Water solubility decreases with increasing chain length
  • Polar nature affects reactivity and solubility

Preparation Methods

Reduction of Carbonyl Compounds

Aldehydes and ketones can be reduced to primary and secondary alcohols respectively.

Grignard Reaction

Grignard reagents react with carbonyl compounds to form alcohols.

Chemical Reactions

Oxidation Reactions

Primary alcohols oxidize to aldehydes and then to carboxylic acids. Secondary alcohols oxidize to ketones.

Dehydration

Alcohols undergo dehydration to form alkenes in the presence of acid catalysts.

Esterification

Alcohols react with carboxylic acids to form esters.

Haloform Reaction

Methyl ketones and alcohols that can be oxidized to methyl ketones undergo the haloform reaction.

Applications

Alcohols have wide-ranging applications:

  • Solvents in industry and laboratories
  • Fuel additives (ethanol in gasoline)
  • Antiseptics and disinfectants
  • Precursors to other organic compounds
  • Beverages and food industry
